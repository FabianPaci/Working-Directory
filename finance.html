Finance

Formulas

  • Simple interest: \(I = prn\)
    • \(I\) = Interest earned
    • \(p\) = Principle amount
    • \(r\) = Rate (%)
    • \(n\) = Number of periods

  • Compound interest: A = P(1 + r)n
    • \(A\) = total Amount
    • \(p\) = Principle amount
    • \(r\) = Rate (%)
    • \(n\) = Number of periods

  • Depreciation: D = P(1 - r)n
    • \(D\) = Depretiated amount
    • \(p\) = Principle amount
    • \(r\) = Rate (%)
    • \(n\) = Number of periods

Examples

  1. $5000 invested for 16 months at 4% simple interest p.a.
    • \(\$5000 \times \frac{16}{12} \times 4\% \)
  2. $8000 invested for 3 years at 4% p.a., compounding annually.
    • \(\$8000(1+4\%)^3 = \$8998.91\)
  3. A car is purchased for $12500 and depreciates at a rate of 18% p.a.. Calculate its value after 3 years.
    • \(\$12500(1-18\%)^3 = \$6892.10\)
